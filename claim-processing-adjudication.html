🧠 What is a Medical Claim?

A medical claim is a bill sent by a healthcare provider to an insurance company asking for payment for services rendered to a patient.

💡 Think of it like:
Restaurant Bill = You get a bill after eating
Medical Claim = Provider sends a bill to insurance after treating patient

🏥 Patient Visit → 📋 Claim Created → 📨 Sent to Insurance → 🔍 Adjudication → 💳 Payment/Denial

⚙️ Claim Processing - Step by Step

What happens from patient visit to payment

📝 Step 1: Charge Entry

  • Patient demographics entered
  • Insurance info verified
  • CPT codes (what was done)
  • ICD codes (why it was done)
  • Charges amount recorded

🔍 Step 2: Claim Scrubbing

  • Auto-check for errors
  • Missing info flagged
  • Code validation
  • Eligibility verified
  • Clean claim = ready to send

⚙️ Claim Processing - Continued

📨 Step 3: Claim Submission

  • Electronic (EDI 837) or Paper (CMS-1500)
  • Sent to Clearinghouse
  • Clearinghouse validates & forwards
  • Goes to Insurance/Payer

📈 Step 4: Payer Receives

  • Insurance logs the claim
  • Assigns claim number
  • Queued for adjudication
  • Standard: paid within 30 days

⚠ Key Term: EDI 837 = Electronic Data Interchange format for claims
CMS-1500 = Paper claim form (professional services)

🔍 What is Claim Adjudication?

The process where the insurance company reviews a claim and decides whether to pay, partially pay, or deny it.

📨 Claim Received → 🔍 Review → ✅ Pay / ❌ Deny / 🟡 Partial

💡 During adjudication, insurance checks:
1. Is patient eligible on date of service?
2. Is the service covered under the plan?
3. Was prior authorization obtained?
4. Is the provider in-network?
5. Does the diagnosis match the procedure?

🔍 Adjudication - How It Works

✅ Clean Claim (Paid)

  • All info correct
  • Patient was eligible
  • Service is covered
  • No prior auth needed
  • Provider in-network
  • Result: Paid in full or allowed amount

❌ Claim Denied

  • Missing/incorrect info
  • Patient not eligible
  • Service not covered
  • No prior authorization
  • Provider out-of-network
  • Result: $0 paid, needs correction

📊 EOB vs ERA

📄 EOB - Explanation of Benefits

  • Sent to PATIENT
  • Explains what was paid
  • Shows patient responsibility
  • Paper or digital letter
  • Shows claim breakdown

💻 ERA - Electronic Remittance Advice

  • Sent to PROVIDER
  • Electronic version of EOB
  • Used for auto payment posting
  • Format: EDI 835
  • Contains denial codes

⚠ In AR Calling: We mostly work with ERA/835 data to post payments and identify denials.

⚠️ What Happens When Denied?

🔄 Denied Claim Action Plan

  • 1. Check denial reason code
  • 2. Review patient records
  • 3. Correct the issue
  • 4. Resubmit or Appeal
  • 5. Follow up in 7-14 days

💬 AR Calling Actions

  • Call payer to check status
  • Request reprocessing
  • Ask for call reference#
  • Document everything
  • Escalate if needed

💡 Remember: Denied does NOT mean dead! Most denials can be recovered through proper follow-up and appeals.

Key Takeaway

Claim Processing = The entire journey from patient visit to payment

Claim Adjudication = The insurance decision-making step inside that journey

💡 As an AR Caller, your job is to:
1. Ensure claims are submitted correctly (Processing)
2. Monitor adjudication results (Paid/Denied)
3. Follow up on unpaid/denied claims
4. Recover maximum revenue for the practice
