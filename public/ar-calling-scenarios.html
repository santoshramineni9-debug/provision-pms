S1: Claim Applied Towards Deductible

DED / BILLPT / SBILL

📋 Example Scenario

Patient: Michael Torres | DOB: 03/15/1985 | Insurance: BCBS Blue PPO | Policy#: XJ-883421 | Provider: Riverside Family Medicine (Dr. Patel) | Claim#: CLM-20260712-0041 | DOS: 05/22/2026 | Billed: $475.00 | Allowed: $380.00 | Paid: $0.00 | Patient Resp: $380.00 (applied to deductible) | Processed: 06/10/2026

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) What is the processed date and claim #?
  • 2) How much was allowed and applied towards deductible?
  • 3) Was it processed in-network or out of network?
  • 4) May I know the patient's total in-network deductible and how much has been met?
  • 5) Do you find any other insurance for this patient?
  • 6) Could you fax/mail me a copy of the EOB?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Bill patient if there is no other insurance listed in system
  • 2) If secondary payer exists, check if we have the EOB. If not, place request for EOB. If we have the EOB, bill secondary payer

💡 Memory Tip: DED = Deductible — patient owes it all until deductible is met. Always check for secondary insurance before billing the patient.

S2: Claim Paid to Provider - Paid Through EFT/Direct Deposit

CIP / CPNP / CNC

📋 Example Scenario

Patient: Sandra Williams | DOB: 11/08/1972 | Insurance: Aetna HMO | Policy#: AET-556789 | Provider: Oak Street Orthopedics (Dr. Kim) | Claim#: CLM-20260803-0127 | DOS: 06/14/2026 | Billed: $1,250.00 | Allowed: $980.00 | Contractual Adj: $270.00 | Paid: $784.00 | Patient Resp: $196.00 | EFT#: EFT-99214 | Paid Date: 07/08/2026

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) Could you tell me the paid date and claim #?
  • 2) What is the allowed amount, contractual adjustment, patient responsibility, paid amount?
  • 3) What is the EFT#/Transaction # and EFT date/Transaction date?
  • 4) Check has been cashed or not and cashed date?
  • 5) Was any procedure code denied?
  • 6) Could you fax me the EOB?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Follow up according to the TAT
  • 2) If not cashed, follow up accordingly

💡 Memory Tip: EFT = Electronic Funds Transfer — payment sent directly to bank. If paid but not posted, verify EFT number and check if cashed.

S3: Claim Paid to Provider - Paid to Incorrect Address

PTWA

📋 Example Scenario

Patient: David Nguyen | DOB: 07/22/1990 | Insurance: UHC Choice Plus | Policy#: UHC-334128 | Provider: Pacific Neurology (Dr. Chang) | Claim#: CLM-20260619-0088 | DOS: 04/03/2026 | Billed: $2,100.00 | Allowed: $1,650.00 | Paid: $1,320.00 | Check#: 774291 | Check Date: 05/29/2026 | Mailed to: Old address - 142 Elm St (practice moved to 890 Oak Ave)

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) Could you tell me the paid date and claim #?
  • 2) What is the allowed amount, contractual adjustment, patient responsibility, paid amount?
  • 3) What is the check # and date on check?
  • 4) Is it a bulk check or single check and whom was the check paid to?
  • 5) What is the mailing address of check and if check cashed or not?
  • 6) Why was it sent to incorrect address?
  • 7) Could you give me your fax # to send you a corrected W9?
  • 8) Was any procedure code denied?
  • 9) Could you fax/mail me an EOB?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Send an email to supervisor to inform client that check # XXX paid on YY to incorrect address still not posted in system

💡 Memory Tip: PTWA = Paid to Wrong Address — check is lost/misdirected. Send corrected W9 to insurance and escalate to supervisor immediately.

S4: Claim Denied as Inclusive/Incidental

COD

📋 Example Scenario

Patient: Rebecca Johnson | DOB: 01/30/1988 | Insurance: Cigna Open Access Plus | Policy#: CIG-771234 | Provider: Summit Surgery Center (Dr. Adams) | Claim#: CLM-20260728-0056 | DOS: 06/01/2026 | Billed: $4,800.00 | Allowed: $0.00 | Paid: $0.00 | Denial: CPT 29881 (knee arthroscopy) deemed inclusive/incidental to CPT 27447 (knee replacement) on same DOS | Processed: 07/05/2026

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) May I have the denial date and claim number?
  • 2) Could you give me the primary procedure?
  • 3) Would you take the claim for reprocessing if a modifier is given over phone?
  • 4) Could you give me the fax # mailing address where we can submit corrected claim to?
  • 5) Could you fax/mail me an EOB?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) If claim was taken for reprocessing, follow up after 30 days or depends upon processing time
  • 2) Else escalate to coding to rebill with a modifier

💡 Memory Tip: COD = Inclusive/Incidental — one procedure "includes" the other. Add a modifier (-59, -XE, etc.) to unbundle and resubmit.

S7: Claim Not on File

  • 1) Do you have any Clearinghouse rejections at your end?

  • 11) Could you check if you received the claim through crossover from Medicare?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Rebill

💡 Memory Tip: CNOF = Claim Not On File — insurance never got it. Get fax# and rebirth. Always check clearinghouse for rejections first.

S8: Claim in Process

CIP

📋 Example Scenario

Patient: Lisa Thompson | DOB: 04/19/1982 | Insurance: UHC Navigate HMO | Policy#: UHC-220847 | Provider: Eastside Pediatrics (Dr. Gupta) | Claim#: CLM-20260730-0112 | DOS: 07/02/2026 | Billed: $285.00 | Status: Claim received 07/05/2026, sent for processing 07/12/2026, still in process — normal 30-day TAT

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) Could you tell me the date when you received the claim?
  • 2) May I have the claim # please?
  • 3) When was the claim sent for processing/reprocessing?
  • 4) How long would it take to process this claim? Reason for delay if any?
  • 5) Are there any supporting documents required causing delay?
  • 6) Is the provider eligible for interest payment due to slow processing?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Follow up according to TAT (Turn Around Time) and place reminder in ReLi

💡 Memory Tip: CIP = Claim In Process — don't panic. Note the TAT, set a reminder, and call back. Ask about interest if TAT is exceeded.

Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) What is the denial date and claim #?

Patient: Laura Jenkins | DOB: 06/22/1984 | Insurance: BCBS State Health Plan | Policy#: BCBS-SHP-77890 | Provider: Metro Ambulatory Surgery (Dr. Foster) | Claim#: CLM-20260705-0037 | DOS: 05/25/2026 | Billed: $12,400.00 | Status: Claim sent for re-pricing to MultiPlan repricing agency on 07/01/2026 | Repricing Ref#: RP-44210 | Repricing Agency: MultiPlan PPO Plus | Processed: 07/20/2026 (still in re-pricing)

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) What is the processed date and claim #?
  • 2) Date sent for re-pricing?
  • 3) Take re-pricing number and name
  • 4) Call repricing agency and know claims status and claim#
  • 5) Could you fax/mail me an EOB?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Work accordingly

💡 Memory Tip: RPRICE = Re-Pricing — a third party (like MultiPlan) is recalculating the allowed amount. Get the repricing agency info and call them directly.

S25: Claim Applied Towards Capitation

CAPITATION

📋 Example Scenario

Patient: Robert Taylor | DOB: 08/30/1975 | Insurance: Health Net Cal Managed Care (Medi-Cal HMO) | Policy#: HN-MC-11234 | Provider: Valley Health Partners IPA (Dr. Sato) | Claim#: CLM-20260718-0082 | DOS: 06/22/2026 | Billed: $450.00 | Allowed: $320.00 | Paid: $0.00 | Status: $320.00 applied towards capitation — provider receives per-member-per-month payment, so individual claim has $0 paid | Processed: 07/15/2026

💬
Questions to Ask Insurance
  • 1) What is the processed date and claim #?
  • 2) Amount which has been processed & applied towards capitation?
  • 3) Could you fax/mail me an EOB?
⚙️
Action to Take
  • 1) Adjust as capitation

💡 Memory Tip: CAPITATION = Provider gets paid per patient per month, not per visit. Claim is zeroed out — just adjust as capitation and move on.
